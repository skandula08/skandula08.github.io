# ROUND 1

The initial idea was to make an ARG, which is an “Alternate Reality Game” Film studios like doing these a lot to promote their upcoming movies, where you convince prospective audiences that the events in their story are real. 
Basically, I got some inspiration from a website called ****<u>[“Mystery Flesh Pit”](https://www.mysteryfleshpitnationalpark.com/)</u>**** and I thought it could be fun to create graphical props, sort of like a movie set, that conveyed the story through the visuals.

I started created miscellaneous graphics, heavy on miscellaneous. 
<br />
<img src="/assets/pyromanimals/misc.png"  width="950px" style="margin: 5px" />
A lot of these sketches and drawings were effectively attempts at creating real world content, like National Parks brochures, google and app alerts and icons, news articles, signs and even thought about creating a fake government bureau all about containing the monsters.

I think the main issue was ultimately that I was stretching out the concept too broadly. I was making all these designs and illustration but they were all over the place. 
Too many different things, too random. 
As fun as the idea was maybe it isn’t meant to be, but I still wanted to do something that was fun with my monster concept.

## Forest Environment
I made a low poly forest environment, two sided with a river in between. There’s a cylinder around it for extra background! I overlaid an image onto the cylinder or a forest fire. Lighting was created with a “sun” object with an orange hue, and I used an HDRI of a smoky sky to complete the forest fire look. Making a properly saturated blue the way i wanted in my illustrations wasn’t working the way I liked and made every model a deep blue instead.

<img src="/assets/pyromanimals/forestenv.png"  width="100%" style="margin: 5px" />

When rendered one side is a burnt brown while the other side is a healthier green, this was mean to show the two stages of the forest gameplay. Part one you fight creatures in a green forest, and in part three you trek through the dying forest to get to the nest!

## Substance Painting

Having never really  3d modeled before, I was struggling to create a door for the bunker with meshes. What I tried instead was substance painting~
After uv unwrapping the cut out hexagonal box that represented the door, I drew onto the faces with a smudgy black brush that made the door look weathered. I also drew into the shadows to simulate sculpted geometry.
The caution tape followed the same principle of simply being stretched cubes that I wrote onto.

<img src="/assets/pyromanimals/substancepainting.png"  width="100%" style="margin: 5px" />



## Shaders and Glowy objects

It took a while to create this lab scene. Once I had these experiment tanks lined up, I wanted them to be ominously glowy. This scene if for part 2, the bunker exploration, at the desk at the back, you can find the secret weapon. 
To make the ominous glow, we add an emission filter to the principal shader, then in the compositing stage, add a “glare” so that anything emitting light gets diffused through the air. Otherwise the render would look like a bright white tubes with no glow.

# Final Thoughts

Overall, I think I was a little all over the place when working on this project. The real struggle was figuring out what to do next when you’ve already made a sketch or came up with an idea. That’s the part about self directed projects that I think I will keep learning.

The real fun came from learning new things! I never worked in 3d before but picking up a new software really helped me more some substantial progress and actualize the things I was thinking in my head! Incidentally learning was the part that helped me start bringing things to completion.
I’m not done with this project, because I have plans to make games with my friends in our future indie studio, so this will continue to be iterated on. Coming up with enemy behavior and the ai algorithm for it will be fun in the future, I know.
